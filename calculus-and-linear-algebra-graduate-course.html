1 General Information

Course Name Abbreviation: MMM009-340181
Credits: 5.00

Each week the students have 3.25hr/w for classes. In addition to that, the students are supposed to work 9.75hr/w on reading material and homework.

1.1 Course Description

This module “Calculus and Linear Algebra for Graduate Students” is a service course consisting of two units: Calculus and Linear Algebra. The topics from these units are introduced with only a high school mathematics as a prerequisite. This means that the students are already familiar with basic ideas/notions of at that level. The course is not a repetition of the high school contents in any way, but rather, it is designed to take the participants further in an optimal way so that they can understand and handle instances of Calculus and Linear algebra.

1.3 Syllabus

Please see the tentative contents below.

Linear Algebra: (8W)

  • Linear Spaces, Matrix Algebra
  • Linear Bases and Dimensions
  • System of Linear Equations
  • Orthogonal Bases and Orthogonal Projections
  • Gram-Schmidt Process
  • Least Square Problems
  • Pseudoinverse
  • Eigenvalue Problems
  • Diagonalization of a Matrix
  • Symmetric Matrices
  • Positive Definite Matrices
  • Singular Value Decomposition
  • Change of Eigenvalues and Singular Values with a Change in the Matrix
  • Special Matrices

Calculus: (6W)

  • Infinite Series
  • Linear Approximations
  • Exponential and Logarithms
  • Simple Optimization Problems
  • Constraints and Lagrange Multipliers
  • Linear System of Equations with Constant Coefficients

1.4 Intended Learning Outcomes

Upon the completion of this module, the participants will be able to

  1. explain, write down and compare fundamental notions of Linear Algebra and Calculus and their implications.
  2. start handling the situations where Linear Algebra and Calculus is used.
