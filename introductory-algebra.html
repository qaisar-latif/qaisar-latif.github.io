1.1 Course Description:

This module “Introductory Algebra” consists of three fundamental units of Mathematics: Group theory, Ring theory and Field theory. The topics from these three units are introduced at a standard undergraduate level. The choice of these contents prepares students to start handling independently some mathematical situations not only in Algebra but also in many other fields of Pure and Applied Mathematics. Of course one needs to inform themselves and explore further. At any rate, this course is a good starting point.

The course include the activities: lecturing, discussions, Q&A sessions, homework and quizzes. Two days are reserved for lecturing and (short) discussions. The third day is reserved for detailed discussions, Q&A sessions and exercises.

Prerequisites: CH-201 Linear Algebra.

1.2 Syllabus

Please see the tentative contents below.

Group Theory: (5W)

  1. Groups and Examples
  2. Mappings
  3. Homomorphisms
  4. Cosets and Normal Subgroups
  5. Permutation Groups
  6. Finite Abelian Groups
  7. Sylow Subgroups
  8. Linear Groups

Ring Theory: (5W)

  1. Rings and Examples
  2. Ideals
  3. Homormorphisms
  4. Quotient Fields
  5. Polynomials Rings
    1. Polynomials and Polynomial Functions
    2. Greatest Common Divisor
    3. Unique Factorization
    4. Principal Rings and Factorial Rings
    5. Symmetric Polynomials

Field Theory: (4W)

  1. Algebraic Extensions
  2. Embeddings
  3. Splitting Fields
  4. Galois Theory
  5. Quadratic and Cubic Extensions
  6. Solvability by Radicals

1.3 Recommended Texts

Serge Lang: Undergraduate Algebra, 3ed, Springer, 2004

1.4 Intended Learning Outcomes

Upon the completion of this module, the participants will be able to

  1. explain, write down and compare fundamental notions of abstract algebra and their implications.
  2. start handling the situations where abstract algebra is used.

2 Homework

Homework deadlines: Tuesdays, before the lecture starts.

Different Homework Problems
Normally, the homework problems for different people are different. There may be some intentional or non-intentional overlaps.
When to submit homework?
You don’t have to wait for the last minute to give your homework. You can submit as soon as you are done with it.
How to submit homework?
You can either submit your homework before the lecture on Tuesday, or send it to me via an email as a single PDF file as an attachment. The copying machines on campus scan for free and do a very good job. In case you are sending a PDF file please choose a “good” name for the file that includes your name and homework number — a couple of examples: intro-algebra-name-hw-#.pdf or intro_algebra_name_hw_#.pdf.
The Algorithm of the homework grading
  1. Send/Submit your homework
  2. I will mark/grade and send/give it back to you.
  3. If there is a revision required, then do it and send/submit the corrected version back to me.
  4. If everything looks good, then we are done, otherwise do as in point 3 above.
